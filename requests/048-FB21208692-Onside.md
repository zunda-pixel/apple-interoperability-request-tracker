## Descriptive Title

Provide App Store–equivalent analytics and install source attribution to third party app
marketplaces without per app SDKs

## Request Type

Interoperability Request

## Feature Name

App Store Connect → App Analytics (specifically the Acquisition, Metrics, and Retention
pages). “Performance metrics” refers to the content of those pages (e.g., Retention Rate,
Sessions per Active Device, Crash Rate), not a separate section.

## Reason for Request

We’re seeking functional equivalence with App Analytics for apps distributed via our alternative
marketplace (Onside)—without per app SDKs. Under the Digital Markets Act (DMA), Apple is
obliged to allow and technically enable the installation and effective use of third-party software
application stores, like Onside (Article 6(4) DMA). In order for effective use of Onside (by
customers, including app developers and advertisers/publishers), access to certain (attribution
and analytics) data is necessary. Such data is available under iOS and through the App Store
Connect but is not available for third party software application stores such as Onside.
Particularly, there are three distinct gaps:
Issue 1 — SDK less Performance parity (sessions/retention/crashes):
App Analytics surfaces Retention Rate, Sessions per Active Device, and Crash Rate without
any App Store SDK embedded in each app. Alternative marketplaces have no access to OS
level telemetry for the apps we distribute; we only learn that an install occurred. Per app SDKs
aren’t equivalent: they impose developer burden, deliver incomplete coverage, and can’t match
OS level completeness, thereby disallowing effective use of Onside.
Issue 2 — Acquisition & attribution parity (store install referrer):
App Analytics provides Acquisition reporting (Impressions, Product Page Views, Conversion
Rate, First Time Downloads, Redownloads, Total Downloads, Installations, Sessions, Active
Devices, Active Last 30 Days, Deletions, In App Purchases, Proceeds, Sales, Paying Users)
sliced by Source / App Referrer / Web Referrer, with campaign links. As a third party store, we
receive no referrer/URL when the user installs the store app from the web, and iOS offers no
handoff from the web store install page → store app, nor shared context between store and
installed apps. Even with our SDK, we cannot attribute sessions/retention to install sources or
provide channel/source and per campaign views equivalent to App Analytics. This does not
enable or allow for the effective use of Onside as a third party software application store.
Issue 3 — Granularity parity (small cohort data):
App Analytics renders slices even for very small cohorts, including when a slice represents a
single device (n=1) (visible in the Acquisition charts’ tooltips). To achieve parity or at least
effective use, third party stores need analytics at the same granularity Apple shows to
developers, rather than stricter suppression that would hide small n slices. This does not
require user level identifiers—only that counts may legitimately equal 1 where App Analytics
shows them.
The issues described above prevent the effective use of Onside as a third party app store.
Therefore, Onside requests Apple to enable/allow for SDK-less performance as well as access
to attribution and analytics data. Apple is obliged to provide Onside with access to such data.
Pursuant to Article 6(8) DMA, Apple is to provide third parties (like Onside) upon their request
with access to Apple’s performance measuring tools and the necessary data for advertisers
and publishers to carry out their own independent verification of the advertisements inventory,
including aggregated and non-aggregated data. In that respect, it is important to note that
Article 6(8) DMA does not only apply to the designated core platform service of ‘online
advertising services’ - which Apple does not offer - but also to “those [services] that are fully
integrated with other core platform services of the same undertaking” such as iOS and the
Apple App Store (recital 58 DMA). Additionally, concerning the analytics data, Apple has to
provide such data upon request with “effective, high-quality, continuous and real-time access
to, and use of, aggregated and non-aggregated data, including personal data, that is provided
for or in the context of the use of the relevant core platform service (Article 6(10) DMA). In light
of these DMA obligations, Onside makes this request.

## Product Description

Onside — an alternative app marketplace for iOS that enables developers to distribute their
apps outside the App Store within the EU. Our goal is to provide developers parity analytics
with what they receive through App Store Connect, so they can make informed product and
business decisions when choosing Onside.

## How the Product Will Use the Feature

• Developer analytics portal: For every app distributed via Onside, show the same Performance
(Retention, Sessions/Active Device, Crash Rate) and Acquisition/Usage metrics (with the same
slices by Source / App Referrer / Web Referrer) that Apple makes available to App Store
developers—without requiring any Onside SDK.
• Campaign management: Let developers generate campaign links (e.g., onside://… or HTTPS
links) and attribute store/product page and app installs to those campaigns, just like App Store
Connect’s Campaigns.
• Quality monitoring: Surface crash rates and stability trends for apps installed via Onside so
developers can triage issues quickly, matching App Store Connect behavior.

## Where the Product Is/Will Be Offered

European Union

## Other Frameworks Evaluated

• Our own telemetry SDK (evaluated): technically possible but not equivalent to Apple’s
approach or in any case does not enable effective use of Onside as a third party app store. It
places integration and privacy review burdens on every developer, produces inconsistent
coverage (many apps will not integrate), and cannot observe store level product page funnels
with the same completeness as OS level pipelines.
• MetricKit / crash reporting (evaluated): these APIs surface data to each app (or its developer)
and still require per app integration. They do not solve store level acquisition or install source
attribution for marketplace flows.
• Ad attribution (e.g., SKAdNetwork or AdAttributionKit) (evaluated): designed for ad
campaigns, not store/product page analytics or marketplace campaign links; it does not
provide App Store–style Acquisition page slices for a store or its catalog. SKAdNetwork
doesn’t work for alternative app marketplaces. AdAttributionKit is not supported by major ad
networks.
• Device side heuristics (evaluated): cannot access install referrer for the store, so we cannot
reliably attribute “web → store install → app install” or generate campaign level reporting
equivalent to App Store Connect.
Because the App Store already enjoys SDK less analytics and acquisition reporting based on
OS controlled features, the only way to achieve effective use of Onside is for Apple to provide
interoperability with those same features to third party marketplaces under Article 6(7) DMA
and to grant the (data) requests made under Articles 6(8) and 6(10) DMA.

## Additional Information

Scope and safeguards we propose (privacy & integrity)
Per install data access via MarketplaceKit’s installVerificationToken:
• Keying: All analytics and attribution for apps installed via our marketplace are addressed by
MarketplaceKit’s installVerificationToken for that app on device installation. Apple’s docs show
this token is passed by the marketplace app in confirmInstall(… installVerificationToken: …) and
is included as a required field in the server restore/update responses. We are asking to use this
same token to retrieve OS collected analytics and attribution for that install.
• Privacy governance: Onside will establish and operate all necessary privacy policies and
disclosures (controller role) for per install analytics/attribution tied to installVerificationToken,
including consent, transparency, retention, and DSR handling.
• Siloing: Telemetry retrieved by installVerificationToken must be scoped to (store, app, device)
and only accessible to the distributing marketplace (Onside) under an entitlement (e.g.,
Marketplace Analytics).
• Event scope: Access to sessions (start/stop/foreground time), installs/updates/deletions, and
crash events/aggregated crash rate sufficient to compute App Analytics’ Performance metrics
and slice by Source / App Referrer / Web Referrer and campaign link IDs.
• Granularity: Counts and slices should match App Store Connect → App Analytics behavior,
including visibility of small cohorts (even with 1 sample) where Apple shows them today.
• Security/integrity: Calls use a server to server API authenticated by our marketplace identity
and the installVerificationToken (or an exchange token derived from it) and rate limited/audited
by Apple.
Success criteria (parity with App Store Connect)
• For apps installed via Onside, developers can see the same metric names, definitions, and
dimensions they see for App Store distribution: Performance, Acquisition, Usage, Sales, and
campaign level reporting, including Source / App Referrer / Web Referrer slices and
configurable campaign links.
• No per app Onside SDK is required for retention, sessions/active device, crash rate, or
acquisition metrics—matching Apple’s SDK less approach for apps distributed by the App
Store.

January 7, 2026, from Apple to Developer:
Thank you again for your request.
We have reviewed your submission and determined that your request falls outside the scope of
Article 6(7) DMA because the functionality to which you seek access (the retrieval of raw
attribution data) is not a feature of iOS or iPadOS. For this reason, we are not moving your
request to the next phase of our interoperability review process. However, we are examining
whether there are additional metrics or data that can be made available to your app
marketplace or developers. We will let you know the outcome of that consideration via
separate outreach.

requested constitutes a feature within the meaning of Article 6(7)), it is not eligible for the
dispute resolution mechanism.
