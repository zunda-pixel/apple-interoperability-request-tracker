## Descriptive Title

Access to OS-level app retention and lifecycle tracking for marketplace-distributed apps

## Request Type

Interoperability Request

## Feature Name

iOS System-Level App Lifecycle Event Detection and Aggregation Capability

## Reason for Request

iOS includes a system-level capability that automatically detects app lifecycle events such as
installation, deletion, and reinstallation. These events are aggregated and presented to
developers through App Store Connect as retention and lifecycle metrics without requiring any
SDK integration.
This request does not seek access to user-level event histories or device identifiers. It seeks
access to the same aggregated lifecycle metrics and reporting functionality that iOS already
provides to App Store developers.
SDK-based alternatives cannot reliably detect lifecycle events such as app deletions, as a
deleted app cannot emit signals. Only the operating system has complete visibility into these
events.
As a result, lifecycle metrics available to App Store developers are complete and consistent,
while metrics available to alternative marketplace developers are inherently incomplete and
non-comparable.
The relevant functionality is therefore the OS-level detection and aggregation of lifecycle events
and their provision to developers, which constitutes a system-level capability controlled by
iOS.

## Product Description

Onside — an alternative app marketplace for iOS that enables developers to distribute their
apps outside the App Store within the EU.

## How the Product Will Use the Feature

Onside will use the OS-level lifecycle tracking to provide developers with retention analytics
equivalent to App Store Connect → App Analytics:
1. Developer analytics dashboard: Onside will present retention metrics and lifecycle data to
developers, enabling them to understand install-to-deletion patterns and make data-driven
decisions — equivalent to what App Store Connect provides.
2. App catalog quality assurance: Onside will use lifecycle data to monitor app quality across
its catalog, identifying apps with abnormal deletion rates or retention problems to ensure
quality for end users.

## Where the Product Is/Will Be Offered

European Union

## Other Frameworks Evaluated

We have evaluated the following alternatives:
1. Third-party analytics SDKs (Firebase, Amplitude, Adjust, etc.): These require per-app SDK
integration by every developer. App Store developers get equivalent metrics without any SDK
— the OS tracks lifecycle events automatically. Requiring SDK integration for alternative
marketplace apps creates an asymmetric developer burden that does not exist for App Store
distribution. Additionally, even if developers integrate these SDKs, the data is sent to the
developer — not to the alternative marketplace. This means Onside has no visibility into
retention and lifecycle patterns across its catalog, making it impossible to ensure the quality of
its app catalog for end users. Furthermore, SDK-based solutions have a fundamental technical
limitation: they cannot reliably detect app deletion events, since a deleted app cannot send
data.
2. Accepting limited lifecycle visibility: Developers could operate without retention metrics. This
perpetuates the interoperability gap: App Store developers have automatic lifecycle tracking;
Onside developers lack equivalent tools.
None of these alternatives provide equivalent functionality to the OS-level lifecycle tracking
available to App Store. Only access to the same system-level feature can eliminate the
developer disadvantage.

## Confidential Treatment

Fully available to other developers

## Additional Information

Privacy Safeguards:
- Lifecycle metrics will be aggregated and anonymized. No individual user identifiers or
personal data will be provided to developers.
- Data scope covers only app lifecycle events (install, deletion, reinstall) — not in-app behavior
or user activity.
- All access would be subject to iOS entitlements, audit logging, and EU GDPR requirements.
Technical Details:
- iOS already tracks app lifecycle events for all installed apps and makes them available
through App Store Connect. The infrastructure exists and is operational.
- Apple can extend access to the same lifecycle tracking for apps distributed through
authorized alternative marketplaces, delivered via API.
Success Criteria:
- Onside developers can access retention and lifecycle metrics without requiring per-app SDK
integration.
- Lifecycle tracking coverage matches what is available in App Store Connect → App Analytics.
- Data is available with equivalent freshness as App Store lifecycle reporting.
