## Descriptive Title

Access to OS-level session and usage metrics for marketplace-distributed apps

## Request Type

Interoperability Request

## Feature Name

iOS System-Level App Performance Metrics Collection and Aggregation Capability

## Reason for Request

iOS includes a system-level capability that automatically collects and aggregates app
performance metrics such as sessions, active devices, and usage patterns for installed apps.
These aggregated metrics are made available to developers through App Store Connect →
App Analytics without requiring any SDK integration.
This request does not seek access to raw event-level data or user-level tracking information. It
seeks access to the same aggregated metrics and reporting functionality that iOS already
provides to App Store developers.
Apps distributed via the App Store benefit from OS-level metrics collection without requiring
any developer action, whereas apps distributed via alternative marketplaces must rely on
optional SDK integrations, resulting in incomplete coverage and non-comparable metrics.
Without access to these metrics, alternative marketplaces cannot monitor app quality or
provide developers with equivalent insights, while Apple retains full visibility into apps
distributed via the App Store.
The relevant functionality is therefore the OS-level collection and aggregation of app
performance metrics and their provision to developers, which constitutes a system-level
capability controlled by iOS.

## Product Description

Onside — an alternative app marketplace for iOS that enables developers to distribute their
apps outside the App Store within the EU.

## How the Product Will Use the Feature

Onside will use the OS-level metrics to provide developers with app performance analytics
equivalent to App Store Connect → App Analytics:
1. Developer analytics dashboard: Onside will present session and usage metrics to
developers, enabling them to monitor app performance and make data-driven decisions —
equivalent to what App Store Connect provides.
2. Retention and engagement tracking: Developers will be able to track retention and
engagement trends for their apps distributed via Onside.

## Where the Product Is/Will Be Offered

European Union

## Other Frameworks Evaluated

We have evaluated the following alternatives:
1. Third-party analytics SDKs (Firebase, Mixpanel, Amplitude, etc.): These require per-app SDK
integration by every developer. App Store developers get equivalent metrics without any SDK
— the OS collects data automatically. Requiring SDK integration for alternative marketplace
apps creates an asymmetric developer burden that does not exist for App Store distribution.
Additionally, even if developers integrate these SDKs, the data is sent to the developer — not
to the alternative marketplace. This means Onside has no visibility into app quality, session
performance, or crash rates across its catalog, making it impossible to ensure the quality of its
app catalog for end users.
2. Accepting limited analytics: Developers could operate without usage metrics. This
perpetuates the interoperability gap: App Store developers have automatic analytics; Onside
developers lack equivalent tools, making Onside a less attractive distribution channel
regardless of other merits.
None of these alternatives provide equivalent functionality to the OS-level metrics collection
available to the App Store. Only access to the same system-level mechanism can eliminate the
developer disadvantage.

## Confidential Treatment

Fully available to other developers

## Additional Information

Privacy Safeguards:
- Metrics will be aggregated and anonymized. No individual user sessions, identities, or device
identifiers will be provided to developers.
- Data scope covers only app performance and usage — not in-app behavior, content
consumed, or behavioral analytics.
- All access would be subject to iOS entitlements, audit logging, and EU GDPR requirements.
Technical Details:
- iOS already collects this data for all installed apps and makes it available through App Store
Connect. The infrastructure exists and is operational.
- Apple can extend access to the same aggregated metrics for apps distributed through
authorized alternative marketplaces, delivered via API.
Success Criteria:
- Onside developers can access app usage and session metrics without requiring per-app SDK
integration.
- Metrics coverage matches the core metrics available in App Store Connect → App Analytics.
- Data is available with equivalent freshness and accuracy as App Store analytics.

May 4, 2026, from Apple to Developer:
We have reviewed your submission and determined that your request falls outside the scope of
Article 6(7) DMA because the functionality to which you seek access - the retrieval of app
performance metrics data - is not a feature of iOS or iPadOS within the meaning of Article 6(7)
DMA. For this reason, we are closing your request and not moving it to the next phase of our
interoperability process.
However, please note that we are examining whether there are additional metrics or data that
can be made available to your app marketplace or developers and we will let you know the
outcome of that consideration via separate outreach.
As this determination is based on a non-technical consideration (i.e., whether what is
requested constitutes a feature within the meaning of Article 6(7)), it is not eligible for the
dispute resolution mechanism.
This concludes our review under Article 6(7) DMA.
