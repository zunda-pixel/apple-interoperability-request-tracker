## Descriptive Title

Access to the system-level background update capability independent of marketplace app
presence

## Request Type

Interoperability Request

## Feature Name

iOS System-Level Background App Update Capability Independent of Store Application
Presence

## Reason for Request

iOS includes a system-level capability that automatically updates installed apps in the
background. This capability ensures that apps remain up to date with the latest features, bug
fixes, and security patches.
For apps distributed via the App Store, this functionality operates independently of any user
interaction with the store. Apps continue to receive updates regardless of whether the user
actively uses the App Store.
By contrast, for apps distributed via alternative marketplaces, the current implementation ties
background update functionality to the presence of the marketplace application on the device.
If the user deletes the marketplace app, all apps distributed through that marketplace stop
receiving updates.
The same user, on the same operating system and device, therefore experiences different
update behavior depending solely on the distribution channel used.
This creates a direct security risk, as apps may stop receiving critical updates and patches
while remaining installed and in use.
Conditioning update functionality on the continued presence of the marketplace application
constitutes a degraded implementation and does not meet the requirement of effective
interoperability under Article 6(7) DMA.
The relevant functionality is therefore the OS-level background app update capability
independent of marketplace application presence, which constitutes a system-level feature
controlled by iOS.

## Product Description

Onside — an alternative app marketplace for iOS that enables developers to distribute their
apps outside the App Store within the EU.

## How the Product Will Use the Feature

Onside will use the system-level background update capability to ensure that apps distributed
through Onside continue receiving automatic updates even if the user deletes the Onside
marketplace app. This way, users are not forced to keep the Onside app installed to maintain
their previously installed apps — the same way App Store apps continue updating regardless
of the user's interaction with the App Store.

## Where the Product Is/Will Be Offered

European Union

## Other Frameworks Evaluated

No application-level workaround exists, as background updates are executed exclusively by
system-level processes and cannot be maintained by third-party applications once they are
removed.

## Confidential Treatment

Fully available to other developers

## Additional Information

Privacy Safeguards:
- Decoupling updates from marketplace app presence does not require any additional user
data collection.
- All existing iOS security checks, code signing, and notarization requirements continue to
apply.
Technical Details:
- The App Store already uses a system-level update capability that is independent of any single
app's presence. The current implementation ties update capability for alternative marketplace
apps to the presence of the marketplace application on the device.
- Removing it requires registering alternative marketplace apps in the OS-level update registry,
the same way App Store apps are registered.
Success Criteria:
- Onside-distributed apps receive automatic background updates indefinitely, even if the
Onside app is deleted.
- Users can delete the Onside app without losing update capability for previously installed
apps.

Thank you for your interoperability request.
It is currently in Phase I - Eligibility review. We will reach out via this forum if we need
clarification of your request.
If we do not reach out for clarification, we will endeavor to provide you with the outcome of the
initial assessment of our Phase I - Eligibility review within 20 working days of the date of your

May 4, 2026, from Apple to Developer:
We have reviewed your submission and determined that your request falls outside the scope of
Article 6(7) DMA. Specifically, your request asks Apple to create a new functionality on iOS that
would enable background updates for apps even when the app marketplace distributing them
has been uninstalled from the device. Background updates for apps distributed on the App
Store are likewise not available once the user has uninstalled the App Store from their device.
For this reason, we are closing your request and not moving it to the next phase of our
interoperability process.
As this determination is based on a non-technical consideration (i.e., whether Apple is legally
obligated to create new features within the meaning of Article 6(7) DMA), it is not eligible for the
dispute resolution mechanism.
This concludes our review under Article 6(7) DMA.
