## Descriptive Title

Enable third-party health and fitness apps to deeplink to iOS Bluetooth Settings

## Request Type

Interoperability Request

## Feature Name

Settings Deep Linking

## Reason for Request

Apple's Fitness app can directly link users to the iOS System Settings Bluetooth tab when
users can connect, reconnect, or troubleshoot Bluetooth heart rate monitors and other devices.
This functionality is achieved through some sort of URL scheme or API that allows these Apple
apps to open Settings directly at the Bluetooth section (not just the main Settings app).
Third-party health and fitness apps that connect to the same types of Bluetooth devices
cannot provide this same seamless user experience. We can only direct users to manually
navigate Settings > Bluetooth, creating unnecessary friction and user confusion, especially
when troubleshooting connection issues.
A publicly available API or URL scheme for third-party apps to deep link to specific sections in
the Settings apps would resolve this.

## Product Description

Our heart rate meditation app connects to Bluetooth heart rate monitors, fitness trackers, and
other Bluetooth devices to provide real-time health metrics and audio feedback.When selecting
the device to use or troubleshooting Bluetooth connection we want to present a button to the
user to directly open the System Settings Bluetooth section to fix their device connection.

## How the Product Will Use the Feature

Our app will use this feature to:
- Direct users to Bluetooth Settings when initially pairing a third party heart rate monitor - Help
users troubleshoot when a previously paired device won't connect - Guide users to unpair/
repair devices when needed - Provide the same seamless experience that the Apple Fitness
app currently enjoys to quickly jump into Bluetooth Settings

## Where the Product Is/Will Be Offered

## Other Frameworks Evaluated

Ja, wir haben alle verfügbaren Alternativen bewerten:
- General Settings URL (UIApplication.openSettingsURLString): This only opens our app's
settings page or the main Settings app, requiring users to manually navigate to Bluetooth
settings - a poor user experience compared to direct linking like Apple Fitness.
- CoreBluetooth Framework: While this handles Bluetooth connections programmatically, it
cannot open the system Bluetooth settings for user-driven pairing or troubleshooting like
Apple's apps can.
- In-app instructions: We currently show screenshots and text instructions for navigating to
Bluetooth settings, but this is cumbersome and inferior to the direct navigation Apple's apps
provide.

## Confidential Treatment

Fully available to other developers

## Additional Information

We're simply asking to open a system settings screen that users can already access manually.
This is about removing unnecessary friction from the user experience and ensuring fair
competition in the health and fitness app market.
The implementation would probably be simple: either making an existing private URL scheme
public or providing a new public API method similar to UIApplication.openSettingsURLString
but for specific settings sections.
This interoperability would benefit all health, fitness, and IoT app developers who work with
Bluetooth devices, creating a better experience for iOS users across the ecosystem.

## Communications with Developer

October 3, 2025, from Apple to Developer:
Thank you for your interoperability request.
It is currently in Phase I - Eligibility review. We will reach out via this forum if we need
clarification of your request.
If we do not reach out for clarification, we will endeavor to provide you with the outcome of the
initial assessment of our Phase I - Eligibility review within 20 working days of the date of your
request, which does not include any public holiday as defined by the European Commission.
October 29, 2025, from Apple to Developer:
Thank you for your request.
We will be removing the functionality for which you request interoperability—directly linking
users to the iOS Settings Bluetooth tab—in the near future. We have determined it should not
be part of the platform. As a result, we have determined that your request falls outside the
scope of Article 6(7) DMA because the requested feature will no longer be available to or used
by Apple services.
For this reason, we are not moving your request to the next phase of our interoperability review
process.
This concludes our DMA Article 6(7) review.
