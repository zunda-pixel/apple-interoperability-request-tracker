## Descriptive Title

Access to the system-level app deletion protection capability for alternative marketplaces

## Request Type

Interoperability Request

## Feature Name

iOS System-Level App Deletion Protection Capability

## Reason for Request

Onside is an alternative app marketplace for iOS operating within the EU under the DMA.
The App Store is integrated into iOS as a system-level application and cannot be fully
uninstalled from the device. While users may remove it from the Home Screen, it remains
present in the operating system and continues to provide core functionality such as app
updates, purchase history, and subscription management.
By contrast, alternative marketplaces can be fully uninstalled like any third-party application.
When removed, they lose all ability to provide updates, manage previously installed apps, or
maintain continuity of service.
As a result, the same user, on the same device, cannot fully remove the App Store’s
functionality, but can fully remove the functionalities of an alternative marketplace, leading to a
structural difference in service continuity that depends solely on the distribution channel used.
This creates both continuity and security risks. If a user deletes an alternative marketplace,
apps previously installed through that marketplace may no longer receive updates, including
critical security patches, despite remaining installed and in use on the device.
This difference is not the result of user preference or product quality, but of system-level
integration decisions made by Apple.
The relevant functionality is therefore the OS-level capability to protect marketplace
applications from accidental removal in a user-controlled manner, which constitutes a system-
level feature controlled by iOS.
Under Article 6(7) DMA, Apple is required to provide effective interoperability with, and access
for the purposes of interoperability to, the same features accessed or controlled via iOS as are
available to Apple’s own services.
This request does not seek to restrict user choice or prevent app removal. It seeks to allow
users to explicitly designate a preferred marketplace as protected from accidental deletion,
ensuring continuity of service while preserving full user control.
This protection would be optional, user-controlled, and revocable at any time through system
settings.
Denying access to this capability results in a degraded implementation that does not meet the
requirement of effective interoperability on equal conditions under Article 6(7) DMA.

## Product Description

Onside — an alternative app marketplace for iOS that enables developers to distribute their
apps outside the App Store within the EU.

## How the Product Will Use the Feature

Onside will use the system-level deletion protection capability to ensure continuity of service
for apps distributed through the marketplace.
Currently, iOS provides system-level protection for the App Store without any user-facing
functionality that allows configuration or replication of this behavior for other applications.
Users cannot fully uninstall the App Store, while alternative marketplaces can be removed
entirely.
Onside requires access to an equivalent capability so that this protection can be applied in a
user-consistent and transparent way.
- Parity with system behavior: Onside will be able to benefit from the same system-level
protection that ensures continuity of service for the App Store.
- User-controlled configuration: Users should be able to explicitly manage protection status for
alternative marketplaces through system settings, ensuring that protection is applied
consistently and transparently across different distribution channels.
- Continuity of updates: Apps distributed via Onside will continue receiving updates even if the
user removes the marketplace from active use.
- Revocability: Users retain the ability to remove protection and fully uninstall Onside or other
alternative marketplaces at any time.
This ensures that users are not forced into asymmetric outcomes where system-integrated
services remain available while alternative marketplaces can be fully removed, breaking the
functionality of installed apps.

## Where the Product Is/Will Be Offered

European Union

## Other Frameworks Evaluated

We have evaluated potential alternatives and concluded that no effective or equivalent
solutions exist.
Any application-level or cloud-based approach cannot preserve app update functionality, as
installation and update processes are controlled exclusively by iOS system APIs.
Once the marketplace application is removed, no third-party mechanism can maintain updates
or continuity of service.
Therefore, only access to the same system-level capability can provide effective
interoperability.

## Confidential Treatment

Fully available to other developers

## Additional Information

Privacy Safeguards:
- Deletion protection would be scoped to the individual marketplace application only
- Users would retain full control through Settings (Manage App Deletion Protection)
- No additional telemetry or user data collection would be enabled
- Protection status would be transparent and user-manageable
Technical Implementation:
- The iOS System-Level App Deletion Protection feature already exists and is in use by the App
Store
- Implementation would follow existing Apple frameworks and security protocols
- No new data collection or processing would be required
- Protection would be enforceable through standard iOS app lifecycle management
Success Criteria:
- Onside can request and receive deletion protection status equivalent to the App Store
- Users can explicitly grant or revoke protection via Settings > Apps > Onside > Deletion
Protection
- Protection prevents accidental and intentional deletion while users are relying on the
marketplace
- Deletion protection does not impede users' ability to uninstall any app they choose (uninstall
remains available in Settings)
- Implementation is auditable and transparent to regulators

## Communications with Developer

April 10, 2026, from Apple to Developer:
Thank you for your interoperability request.
It is currently in Phase I - Eligibility review. We will reach out via this forum if we need
clarification of your request.
If we do not reach out for clarification, we will endeavor to provide you with the outcome of the
initial assessment of our Phase I - Eligibility review within 20 working days of the date of your
request, which does not include any public holiday as defined by the European Commission.
May 4, 2026, from Apple to Developer:
We have reviewed your submission and determined that there is parity between the App Store
and alternative app marketplaces, such as Onside, in terms of access to the functionalities
described in your request. In particular, any app marketplace or the App Store set as the
default app for app installation on iOS has the same protections from deletion, which are
controlled by the system without involvement of the app.
For this reason, we are closing your request and not moving it to the next phase of our
interoperability process. This concludes our review under Article 6(7) DMA.
If you have additional questions, you may reach out to us through Apple Developer Forums
(https://developer.apple.com/forums/), Feedback Assistant (https://developer.apple.com/
