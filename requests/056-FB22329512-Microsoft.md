## Descriptive Title

Request to support multiple external devices for iOS notification interoperability.

## Request Type

Interoperability Request

## Feature Name

iOS Notifications – third party device access (ANCS / Accessory notifications)

## Reason for Request

For an iPhone user today, notifications are delivered concurrently across multiple Apple
devices. When a user owns an iPhone, a Mac, and an Apple Watch, notifications appear on
macOS and on the Apple Watch at the same time, without requiring the user to choose
between devices or reconfiguring settings. This multi-device notification model enables users
to stay informed and responsive regardless of which device they are actively using.
Third-party platforms are unable to offer a comparable experience for iPhone users who also
use a Windows PC and locking them out of the best experience possible available on macOS.
Today, third-party accessories can access only a limited subset of iOS notifications over
Bluetooth Low Energy using the Apple Notification Center Service (ANCS). This results in
significantly reduced experience compared to Apple’s own devices. Only Apple Watch users
can receive notifications containing images, attachments, and custom app icons; interact with
notifications (for example replying or taking action); and control which apps notifications
appear on the connected device.
With Apple’s new Accessory Notifications framework, available to developers starting with iOS
26.3 beta and enabled only in EU, the framework allows an app to register for receiving user
notifications and relay them to a connected external device. However, the current
implementation restricts users to selecting only one external device to receive notifications at a
time. While this model may align with smartwatch-only assumptions, it does not reflect how
users work across multiple devices.
As a result, an iPhone user who owns both a smartwatch and a Windows PC must choose
between receiving notifications on their watch or on their PC. Notifications cannot be delivered
to both concurrently; unlike the experience users already have with macOS and Apple Watch.
Switching between devices requires manual, repetitive configuration, which makes multi-device
usage impractical.
We are requesting Apple’s help to enable support for multiple concurrently configured external
devices to receive notifications. This would allow notifications on a Windows PC to coexist with
notifications on a smartwatch, mirroring the experience iPhone users already have with macOS
and Apple Watch. This capability cannot be achieved by third parties without changes to
platform behavior and supported system interfaces and therefore requires Apple’s involvement
to deliver an effective interoperability solution.

## Product Description

Windows, a general-purpose personal computing platform used globally for productivity,
communication, and multi-tasking across consumer and enterprise scenarios.

## How the Product Will Use the Feature

Windows would use interoperable notification access to display iOS notifications on a PC in a
way that complements, rather than replaces, notifications delivered to other connected devices
such as smartwatches.
This includes:
• Receiving notifications that may contain rich content, where supported by the platform
• Allowing users to view and manage notifications while actively working on their PC
• Coexisting with other connected devices (e.g. smartwatches), without requiring users to
disable or disconnect those devices to enable notifications on PC
The intent is to support realistic multi-device usage patterns, where users commonly rely on
more than one connected device at the same time.

## Where the Product Is/Will Be Offered

European Union

## Other Frameworks Evaluated

Yes.
• ANCS provides limited notification metadata and does not support parity with the Apple
Watch notification experience.
• The Accessory Notifications framework introduced in iOS 26.3 improves access but enforces
a single-external-device model, which prevents simultaneous notification delivery to multiple
connected devices.
There is no alternative Apple-provided framework or documented approach that enables
multiple third-party devices to receive notifications concurrently. This limitation cannot be
addressed by third parties without updates to iOS.

## Confidential Treatment

Fully available to other developers

## Additional Information

Apple’s current notification architecture already demonstrates that rich, interactive notifications
can be delivered across multiple devices in a way that preserves user control, privacy, and
consistency. However, existing mechanisms for third-party devices are limited in ways that do
not reflect how users actually work across devices today.
Modern user workflows increasingly involve multiple connected devices operating
simultaneously, such as a smartwatch and a PC. Restricting notification delivery to a single
external device at a time introduces unnecessary user friction and makes it difficult to support
practical multi-device scenarios.
We request that Apple update iOS to support configuring more than one external device to
receive notifications concurrently. Doing so would enable notifications on a PC to coexist with
notifications on other connected devices, rather than requiring users to choose between them,
and would result in a more consistent and usable cross-device notification experience.

April 27, 2026, from Apple to Developer:
We have reviewed your submission and determined that your request appears to be covered
by the European Commission's specification proceeding in case DMA.100203, insofar as it
concerns iOS notifications. On March 19, 2025, the European Commission issued a decision in
those proceedings. You can find the measures specified by the European Commission here,
https://ec.europa.eu/commission/presscorner/detail/en/ip_25_816.
Apple is therefore moving your request to Phase III of the interoperability request process. We
believe Apple's work implementing the European Commission's decision will address the
functionalities concerned by your request. As required by that decision, we expect to complete
that work by June 2026, which is a significant engineering effort that started well before now,
and will proceed on the timeframes set forth in the EC decision for shipping the solution.
A solution to your interoperability request regarding iOS Notifications shipped on December 15,
2025, in an iOS 26.3 beta release in the EU for developer testing. The solution will enable a
third-party accessory to receive iOS system notifications using the Accessory Notifications
framework. Once you use this framework to opt into notification forwarding, iOS will identify
your accessory through the reference you receive from AccessorySetupKit, and will then
prompt a user using your accessory for permission to forward notifications to the accessory
from all, or just some, of the apps that the user chooses.
For more information on the solution that is available in beta, please visit
https://developer.apple.com/download/all/ and search for "Accessory Notifications SDK
26.3.0."
We will endeavor to ship the final solution by June 2026. When the solution ships in a final
release, this will conclude our work on your interoperability request, and we will close your
request as complete. If you have any questions on the solution that shipped in a beta release
for developer testing, please reply to this communication and we will ensure that we direct
assistance appropriately.
May 8, 2026, from Developer to Apple:
Thank you for the follow-up. After reviewing the response, we don't believe it addresses the
core of our interoperability request, and we'd like to clarify the ask.
We are requesting that an iPhone be able to act as a companion to a Windows PC concurrently
with an external device (such as a watch). Basically, it is the same multi-companion pattern
that already exists today between iPhone, Apple Watch, and Mac. On macOS, a paired Apple
Watch and the iPhone receive the same notifications with no requirement that the user choose
one or the other. That parallel companion model is what we want to extend to Windows.
The gap is specifically about single vs. multiple device connectivity, not about any individual
API surface. To recap the request: Parity of companion-device treatment, so that a Windows
PC paired to an iPhone can:
Receive the same notification stream that Apple Watch and Mac receive today, concurrently
with Watch (not as a substitute for it).
Coexist with an active Apple Watch (or any other device) without either side being downgraded
or disconnected.
We are asking that the simultaneous multi-companion model that iOS already implements
internally be made available to a Windows PC companion.
