## Descriptive Title

Request for On-Device Interoperability API: Example: one sec × Structured Integration

## Request Type

Interoperability Request

## Feature Name

HealthKit Data Sharing, Calendar Access, Reminders Access, App Groups

## Reason for Request

While our Structured / one sec integration already exists today and is actively used by
thousands of users, it unfortunately depends on cloud-based data exchange to synchronize
Structured’s local task data with one sec’s intervention logic.
This approach, however, goes directly against both our and Apple’s privacy principles:
- Even minimal data about a user’s tasks must currently leave the device to reach the other
app.
- Users lose the certainty that the integration works fully offline, causes trouble during bad
internet connections.
- Both developers must maintain backend infrastructure solely for this one-device use case.
- The integration becomes slower, more complex, and less reliable.
We would strongly prefer to perform all communication on-device, securely and privately —
exactly as Apple encourages developers to do in so many other contexts (e.g., HealthKit).
Apple already provides powerful privacy-preserving data-sharing frameworks, such as:
- HealthKit, which allows apps to share user health data on-device with clear user consent.
- Calendars & Reminders APIs, enabling apps to access and contribute to personal scheduling
data under strict system mediation.
- App Groups, which allow local data sharing between multiple apps by the same developer.
- …
However, App Groups cannot currently be shared across multiple individual developers, which
prevents responsible collaborations like ours from functioning in a truly private, local way.
We are therefore requesting Apple to extend this capability — under strong privacy and
developer controls — so that independent apps can collaborate on-device without any server
involvement.

## How the Product Will Use the Feature

We propose an extension of App Groups that allows one developer to securely share a subset
of local data with a trusted third-party app, after explicit user consent.
Concept
- App A (Structured) can authorize specific third-party apps (identified by bundle IDs, e.g.
wtf.riedel.one-sec) to access a restricted App Group.
- App A can store a minimized, local task database or plist in this shared container —
containing only essential task metadata such as titles and timestamps, but no sensitive or
personal notes.
- App B (one sec) can read and write this shared data on-device, with user consent, to provide
richer, context-aware experiences.
- All access is mediated by iOS, and revocable by the user at any time.
Example Flow
1. The user enables the “Structured Integration” inside one sec.
2. iOS presents a native consent dialog: “Allow one sec to access data from Structured on this
device?” [Allow] [Don’t Allow]
3. one sec can then locally read upcoming tasks and include them contextually in its
interventions — for example, reminding the user:
“You still have two tasks planned for today in Structured: ‘Finish report’ and ‘Call Anna.’ Would
you really like to open Instagram right now?”
All of this happens entirely offline, on-device, and under strict user control.
Privacy and Security
- User consent is explicit and revocable at any time.
- Optional developer control ensures only whitelisted apps can request access.
- On-device only: no (background) network access.
- Minimal data model: only lightweight, non-sensitive task metadata shared.

November 10, 2025, from Apple to Developer:
We have reviewed your submission and determined that your request falls outside the scope of
Article 6(7) DMA. Your request asks Apple to create new functionality on iOS or iPadOS
(specifically, an on-device interoperability API to enable independent third party apps to share
data) rather than make existing features available to your services or hardware. As noted in
your request, App Groups facilitates data sharing only between multiple installed apps created
by a single developer. Additionally, your request identifies other features — namely, HealthKit
Data Sharing, Calendar Access, and Reminders Access — that rely on specific API frameworks
that would not enable your requested functionality. For these reasons, we are not moving your
request to the next phase of our interoperability process.
This concludes our review under Article 6(7) DMA.
Dispute Resolution
You have the opportunity to initiate a dispute resolution mechanism regarding your
interoperability request under Article 6(7) of the Digital Markets Act (DMA).
If you wish to appeal Apple’s decision to reject your interoperability request on technical
grounds, you will have 15 working days (not including any public holiday as defined by the
European Commission) from receiving this communication to initiate the mechanism.
Timing
To initiate the process, please respond to this communication in Feedback Assistant by
indicating you are initiating the dispute resolution mechanism, and include a brief written
statement setting out the grounds for doing so. The dispute resolution mechanism can consist
of two stages.
Internal Review Mechanism
The first stage is an internal review by an Interoperability Request Review Board (IRRB), made
up of senior Apple members. The IRRB will issue a reasoned written decision within 30 working
days of receiving your brief written statement, and communicate that decision to you, Apple,
and the European Commission without delay. The IRRB may decide to overturn Apple’s initial
decision, refer it back for new consideration, or reject your appeal. The 30 working-day
deadline may be extended if the IRRB seeks guidance from the European Commission. This
stage is free of charge to you.
The IRRB’s written decision will be kept confidential, subject to the publication by Apple of an
aggregated summary of the outcome of the decision by the IRRB. Apple will provide you the
opportunity to review the aggregated summary for the purpose of ensuring that it does not
contain any information that you consider confidential.
Conciliation
If you are not satisfied with the IRRB written decision, you may initiate an external non-binding
technical expert review process (“conciliation”) within 15 working days of notification of the
IRRB’s decision. You will be provided with more information regarding conciliation once you are
eligible for it.
